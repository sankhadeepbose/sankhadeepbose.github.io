Research Interests

Thermodynamics of Real Systems

I investigate how real, nonhomogeneous systems reach thermodynamic equilibrium. Using classical Molecular Dynamics, I analyze how these systems — from nanometer to macroscopic scales — exhibit spatial variations in intensive variables that diverge from predictions for infinitely large ideal systems. This challenges standard equilibrium thermodynamics, revealing a more significant role for surface and boundary effects than previously recognized.

Phase Transitions of Finite Systems

I study first-order phase transitions of finite systems, examining how surface-induced nonhomogeneities, evaporation kinetics, and finite-size effects influence their thermodynamic and dynamical behavior. Using atomistic simulations, I quantify how the classical thermodynamic signatures of phase transitions are modified in these systems.
